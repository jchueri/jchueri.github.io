## Short Bio (50-70 words)

Petter Törnberg is Associate Professor in Computational Social Science at the University of Amsterdam and head of the AI, Culture & Society research unit at ILLC. His research investigates how digital platforms and AI reshape political communication, misinformation, polarization, and governance. He is a recipient of NWO VENI and VIDI grants and author of *Intimate Communities of Hate* and *Seeing Like a Platform*.

## Extended Bio

Petter Törnberg is an Associate Professor in Computational Social Science at the University of Amsterdam and Head of the AI, Culture & Society research unit at ILLC. His work sits at the intersection of digital politics, platform governance, AI, and social theory. He combines computational methods with conceptual analysis to study how social media and AI transform democratic institutions, urban life, and political conflict.

He has published in journals including *PNAS*, *New Media & Society*, *Big Data & Society*, and *The International Journal of Press/Politics*, and is the author of the books *Intimate Communities of Hate* (Routledge, 2024) and *Seeing Like a Platform* (Taylor & Francis, 2025). His research has been covered by outlets including *The Guardian*, *Ars Technica*, and *New Scientist*.
