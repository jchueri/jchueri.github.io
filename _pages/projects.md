## Current Projects

### (Re)distributive Politics after the AI Revolution (REPAIR)
- **Funding:** European Research Council (ERC) Starting Grant
- **Period:** 2025-2030
- **Budget:** approximately EUR 1.5 million
- **Role:** Principal Investigator
- **Focus:** How AI-driven labor market disruption reshapes public preferences, political conflict, and welfare state reform trajectories across OECD countries.

### European Welfare States and the Challenge of Platform Work
- **Institutional base:** University of Lausanne (IDHEAP)
- **Role:** Co-Principal Investigator
- **Focus:** How welfare state institutions shape platform work dynamics and how platform labor pressures social protection systems.

### Welfare State Transformation in the Age of Automation and Digitalization
- **Funding:** Swiss National Science Foundation (SNSF)
- **Start:** 2023
- **Focus:** Political and policy responses to technological change in labor markets, with emphasis on welfare institutions and distributive conflict.

## Ongoing Research Themes

- Welfare chauvinism and migrant deservingness
- Populist radical-right parties and redistribution
- Public opinion on social policy in changing labor markets
- Comparative politics of AI and welfare state adaptation
